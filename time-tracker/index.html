Methodology & caveats

How the numbers are built

  • Calendar: leya@leyapm.com events. Meetings (>1 attendee) vs heads-down (solo blocks) are split. Per-client tag comes from event title and attendee email keyword matches.
  • Granola: Meetings recorded in Granola but not on Calendar are added back via the granola_extra bucket. Calendar wins on overlap.
  • Claude Code: Active minutes per session from transcript timestamps, capped at 10-min idle gaps. Rose's automated runtimes are excluded.
  • Gmail (estimated): 4 minutes per message Leya sent across her three sending accounts.
  • Drive (estimated): 3 minutes per Drive file last-modified by Leya, excluding (a) Granola auto-uploads, (b) folders, and (c) files modified during an active Claude Code session window (those minutes are already counted in the Claude bucket).

Sub-attendee meetings split across clients

A meeting with Kathleen, Kelly, Aryana, or Lauren is billed equally across that sub's current clients (per registries/sub_clients.json). Subs without active client assignments (onboarding) route to People Ops instead.

Known v1 limits

  • Gmail estimated minutes cannot be attributed per-client without thread reading; they land in Other internal.
  • Client tagging is keyword-based; if a meeting title and attendee list miss the rules, it falls through to "untagged" and shows up under Other internal.
  • The Drive estimate cannot capture editing inside files Leya opened but never re-saved during the day.
