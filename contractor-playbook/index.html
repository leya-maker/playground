About LPM Consulting

The Strategic Mirror Approach

My approach is what I call the Strategic Mirror. I help organizations see what's actually true about their fundraising, their data, their relationships, and their potential. That clarity creates the conditions for better decisions. When you're working with me, you're part of that process.

What We Believe

Integrity

We say what we see. We deliver what we promise. We keep confidences. We don't perform trust; we earn it.

Clarity

We explain things plainly. We ask the questions that matter. We surface what's actually happening, not what sounds good in a report.

Community-Centric

We don't use donor-centric language. We believe that fundraising is about connecting passionate partners and donors with the organizations that are best poised to make that work. We center the community that is being impacted, never the donor that is funding that work. Learn more about Community-Centric Fundraising.

What We Do

Fundraising Strategy. We help nonprofit clients understand their supporter ecosystem, build cases for support, and design pipeline development plans. Your work may include research, analysis, or drafting that feeds into these strategies.

Development Consulting. We embed alongside client teams to strengthen their fundraising operations, from staffing structure to gift processing systems. You may support data work, project management, or deliverable production depending on the engagement.

Data Analysis. Gift trend analysis, wealth screening, pipeline health, retention metrics. If you're supporting data work, accuracy is everything. When in doubt, ask before submitting.

Interim and Advisory Support. I serve as a strategic advisor and sometimes step into leadership gaps for clients. Your work supports the deliverables, research, and operations behind those advisory relationships.

Training and Coaching. We run cohort programs and facilitate workshops for nonprofit development teams. You may support curriculum development, materials production, or facilitation logistics.

How Leya Works

Direct Communication

Be clear and be kind. Lead with the point, but lead with empathy too. I value directness because it respects everyone's time, and I value warmth because that's how trust gets built. Communicate with context, be concise, and surface the key takeaway or next step up front.

Quality and Ownership

I notice details. Proofread before presenting anything. Before submitting, ask yourself: would I feel confident handing this to a client as-is? If something is wrong or you make a mistake, own it, then fix it or ask for help. Ownership matters more than perfection. I prioritize completion and clarity over speed. I'd rather have one task done thoroughly than three halfway finished.

Proactive, Not Passive

If you see a way to improve a system or process, note it and share. If something feels unclear, propose two options and your recommendation. Communicate progress even when incomplete. I prefer visibility over silence.

Core Operating Principles

Notion is the source of truth. If it's not in Notion, it doesn't exist. Tasks, deadlines, assignments. All there. Check Notion before asking what you should be working on.

Google Drive is where documents live. Strategy plans, client files, drafts, research. Organized by client. Always pull the latest version before you start working.

Gmail is for client communication. I use leya@leyapm.com. Keep client conversations in email so we have a record.

Granola captures and transcribes every meeting. After client calls, Granola processes the transcript and surfaces action items, decisions, and follow-ups. Each client folder in Google Drive has a "Meetings" subfolder where Granola automatically uploads transcriptions. Refer to these whenever you need context on recent conversations.

Billing and Time Tracking

Contractors bill monthly. Track your hours by client. Be accurate. If your contract has a maximum number of hours, or we've discussed an estimated hours budget for a project, flag it for me when you're approaching half of the allocated hours and think you may need more than planned.

My philosophy is always to do the job well and get the job done right. We can be flexible on hours as needed. But I need to know early.

The AI System

LPM runs an AI agent system that handles meeting follow-ups, task routing, briefings, and client intelligence updates. You don't need to understand the full architecture. You just need to know how it shows up in your work and how to use it yourself.

What Runs Automatically

Here's what you'll see in practice:

  1. Meeting Follow-ups

    After every client meeting, the system processes the Granola transcript and creates a follow-up email draft, proposes Notion tasks, and flags content angles. Leya approves everything via iMessage before anything is created or sent.

  2. Monday Briefings

    Every Monday morning, a briefing is generated with the week's calendar, active tasks across all clients, and strategic priorities. This drives the week's work. Sometimes Leya will forward this on to you and ask you to take on particular elements.

  3. Client Intelligence Updates

    Every client has a client intelligence file in Google Drive. These files include key contacts, scope of work context, big learnings, and status of project developments. They are the single source of truth for each client. After meetings, client intelligence reference files in Drive are updated with new information, decisions, and context.

How This Affects Your Work

You will receive Notion tasks routed by the AI system. They come from meeting follow-ups and have been reviewed and approved by Leya before you see them.

If a task feels unclear or seems misrouted, flag it to Leya immediately.

Client intelligence files in Drive get updated automatically after meetings. If you're working on a client deliverable, always pull the latest version of the client intelligence file first. The file dates matter. Each client folder also has a Meetings subfolder where Granola automatically uploads meeting transcripts for reference.

Installing the Kit

Installation takes about five minutes. Leya will share a Google Drive folder with your skill files. There are two paths depending on how you use Claude:

Option A: Claude Code (CLI)

  1. Download the skill folder

    Download the shared LPM Contractor Skills Kit folder from Drive to your computer.

  2. Run the install script

    Open your terminal, navigate to the downloaded folder, and run: bash install-skills.sh

    This copies the skill files to Claude's skill directory. You'll see a confirmation for each skill installed.

  3. Verify it works

    Open a new Claude conversation and type: "What LPM skills do you have access to?" Claude should list the installed skills. Try: "Run a brand check on the phrase: We are a donor-centered organization." Claude should flag "donor-centered" immediately.

Option B: Claude.ai (Web)

  1. Create an LPM project

    Log in to claude.ai, click Projects in the sidebar, and create a new project called "LPM".

  2. Add skill files as project documents

    For each skill folder in the kit, open the SKILL.md file and add its contents as a new project document. Also add any client reference files (like apex.md) as project documents.

  3. Verify it works

    Start a new conversation within the LPM project and ask: "What LPM skills do you have access to?" Claude should recognize the skills. Try: "Run a brand check on the phrase: We are a donor-centered organization." It should flag "donor-centered" immediately.

Not sure which to use? Ask Leya. Either path works.

Keeping Skills Updated

Leya maintains and updates all skills centrally. When meaningful changes happen, you'll receive a notification with a link to pull the latest version from the shared Drive folder. Run the same install script to update. It overwrites the old files and takes less than a minute.

The rule for AI-generated work: Always review AI-generated content before it goes to Leya. The AI system supports your work. It does not replace your judgment, your expertise, or your eye for detail.

Your First Two Weeks

Step 1: Get Oriented

Confirm your access to Notion, Google Drive, and Gmail. Read this playbook fully. Set up your Claude account and install the LPM Contractor Kit.

Step 2: Learn the Clients

Read the client intelligence files for your assigned clients. Review the Meetings subfolder in each client's Drive folder for recent transcripts. Get familiar with the Notion board for your clients.

Step 3: Understand the Systems

Walk through how tasks flow: meetings generate transcripts, transcripts generate follow-ups and tasks, tasks land in Notion, deliverables live in Drive. Ask Leya to walk you through anything that's unclear.

Begin with your assigned tasks. Flag questions immediately. Check in with Leya at least once during the week to confirm you're oriented.
